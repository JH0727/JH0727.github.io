Relationship between inequality and economic growth

Aim of the project

The aim of this project is to examine the relationship between inequality and growth.
To thoroughly investigate the relationship, I tried to look at various time periods
in various countries around the world.

Specifically, I aimed to depict charts under three different themes. In the first theme,
I tried to focus on how the inequality affects the economic growth in two countries:
Korea and Philippines. In the second theme, I attempted to inspect the relationship
between inequality and growth rate more precisely by using datasets from several
countries and performing regressions using them. For the last theme, I aimed to survey
how economic growth, derived by technological advancement, has impacted the income inequality.

Charts

Theme 1. How inequality affects growth: Korea vs Phillipines

Theme 2. How Income inequality and Land inequality affects growth

Theme 3. How technological advancement affects income inequality

Data used

Chart 1:

Datasets for GDP and Gini index are collected from the World Bank. I used Excel to
sort the countries and years that I want to look at. I used the most recent data
available. Looking at the map, the level of GDP in each country is distinguished
using a color scheme and Gini index is shown using the “tooltip” feature in Vega-lite.

Chart 2,3 (Theme 1):

The datasets are from World Bank and WID except for the Korea’s Gini index in Chart
2. Korea’s Gini index is scraped from Kang’s (2001) paper using Camelot and Pandas
(Colab note ). I converted the per capita GDP dataset into long form using Stata which
was originally in wide form for Chart 3.

Chart 4,5(Theme 2):

Datasets for the regressions were collected from four different papers written by
Fields (1989), Jain (1975), Taylor and Hudson (1972), and Summers-Heston (1984). I
collected GDP data in 1960 and 1985 to calculate the growth rate using the relevant
formula in Excel. For the regressions, I used Matplotlib in Google Collaboratory and
regression feature in Vega-lite to present the graphs (Colab note ).

Chart 6, 7, 8(Theme 3):

I used FRED API to collect dataset for GDP in Research and Development (R&D)
sector and Gini index of USA in Chart 6. I used “For” looping to get both GDP
and Gini in a JSON form(Colab note ). Because the scale of GDP
and Gini were different, I plotted two y-axes using Vega-lite. For the missing
Gini values, I used linear filling function in Excel. In Chart 7, I scraped top 10
countries from Wikipedia who had the most R&D expenditures in recent years (mostly
in 2019) using BeautifulSoup. Also, I used “If-else” statement and “For” looping to
only scrape the part I needed (Colab note ). Then, in Chart 8, I used
WorldBank API to collect the Gini data for these 10 countries (Colab note ). I
added the world average Gini baseline to compare these countries with the other parts of the world (I averagedall the Gini coefficient in 2020 using Excel to get the world
average Gini).

Challenges during data cleaning process

There were not many challenges in terms of data cleaning since there were many
resources available on the internet that I could refer to. However, there were
two major challenges which was kind of time consuming. First, when depicting the
map, the names of some countries in the dataset did not match the names in the
base map, in which, some part of the map didnot appear. For example, in the
World Bank dataset, Russia was written as “Russian Federation”.To solve this
problem, I used “matching” command in Excel to find out the mismatching
countries.Secondly, the data for Gini index was insufficient especially for the
older time periods. In order to fill the missing values, I scraped the old
papers that contained the missing valuesor manually typed it in. If there
were still missing values, I used the linear filling function in Excel.

Conclusion

Looking at the charts, it can be concluded that inequality impedes the growth.
Reversely, growth derived from technological advancement may deepen the inequality,
but it is not always the case. Charts in theme 1 indicates that the more inequal
the country is, the less likely the country will grow. Charts in theme 2
demonstrates that inequality is negatively correlated to growth. Charts intheme 3
shows two points of view. Chart 6 implies that R&D (I assumed R&D as an index for
technological advancement) fosters growth and increases inequality. Whereas chart 7
and 8 impliesthat there is no significant causation between R&D and inequality. (To
prove that there is a certain relationship, the Gini index of the top 10 countries
should be significantly high thanthe World average. But this is not the case.)
